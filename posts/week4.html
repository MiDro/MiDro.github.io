Week 4

Monday June 12, 2017

 The next step would be to find a server that would be able to meet our requirements for the project. We would need a quad-core processor and 6-8 GB of RAM. We have a Google Sheet that organizes our findings. We assigned one team member to making and designing footprints for the PE Stone Board. The other members of the team were assigned with completing the Django tutorial for application development in the future. Mario helped us understand some of the hidden limitations of the project, which also helped us finish the first iteration of the circuit for the sensors.

Tuesday June 13, 2017

 We drew up the engineering schematics for the the sensor board. We also researched antennas for the board. Some interns worked on finishing programming tutorials. Also, we were able to update the midro.github.io site to make it more organized and visually appealing. Additionally, we started making the rough web app using Django. It will display the trash levels and more information. Below is the layout of the overall project that we are trying to accomplish.

Wednesday June 14, 2017

 We managed to compile code onto the development module and learned how to activate the clocks on the microcontroller. The interns continued their programming tutorials and searched for hardware, specifically, a microcontroller that runs on 3.3 volts. The interns were also able to successfully update the Django site for the project with an info section that organizes information about each individual trash can, such as fill level and location. They were also able to switch the google maps icon to a trash icon instead of a red pin.

Thursday June 15, 2017

We were able to pop out in the morning to watch the Golden State Warriors parade celebrating their 2017 NBA champion status. Go Warriors! After coming back, we began to figure out how to connect to a development board we received from Paradox Engineering. This board contains the PE.STONE module which we will use to connect to San Leandro's 6LoWPAN network. Additionally, we began planning the PCB (printed circuit board) to custom order manufactured. We also set up the software toolchain, which is a set of tools to compile the components to run the Paradox board, but ran into bugs.

Friday June 16, 2017

Our morning meeting with people from the city, Paradox, and even in Italy proved very useful. Mario, our liason with Paradox, answered questions pertaining to the board. We visited PhaseSpace to get some further help from James, our main mentor and largest tool supplier. He helped us find creative ways to connect to the STONE around the sensors already on Paradox's development board.
